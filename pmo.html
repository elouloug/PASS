Practice areas  /  Project Management Office

The discipline that turns intent into delivery.

A well-run PMO is not a reporting function — it is the operating system of a complex program. We design, stand up, turn around, and run PMOs that give leadership a single source of truth and give programs the cadence they need to move.

What a PMO is for

Most failed programs do not fail for lack of vision. They fail because the gap between strategic ambition and weekly execution becomes too wide for leadership to close. The PMO closes that gap.

Done well, a PMO is lean, decision-oriented, and indispensable: it gives the steering committee the three or four things they need to act on, removes the dependencies that block delivery, and surfaces the risks that matter before they become issues. Done poorly, it becomes a reporting tax on the teams doing the real work.

We design PMOs around outcomes, not artefacts. The cadence, the governance, the reporting, the tooling — all of it is calibrated to the program it serves, not lifted from a methodology.

How we engage

PASS supports clients across the full PMO lifecycle — from establishing the function for a new program to recovering one in difficulty, and from running the PMO as a service to embedding the capability and stepping away.

i.
PMO setup
Standing up a fit-for-purpose PMO for a new program — governance design, workstream architecture, RAID and dependency framework, financial tracking, reporting cadence, and steering committee operating model. Live within weeks, not quarters.
ii.
PMO turnaround
Rapid diagnostic and reset for programs where delivery has stalled, reporting has lost credibility, or governance has broken down. We restore decision velocity and rebuild leadership confidence — typically within a 30-to-60-day reset window.
iii.
Interim PMO leadership
Senior PMO leadership embedded into the client organisation for the duration of a critical program — running the office, chairing the cadence, owning the deliverables, and bridging to a permanent hire when the moment is right.
iv.
Capability build & exit
PMO capability built and handed over to a permanent internal team. Frameworks, templates, reporting, and operating rituals embedded; people coached; and a clear exit point agreed at the start of the engagement.

Our approach

Three principles that distinguish a PASS PMO from a process-led one.

i.

Decision velocity over reporting volume

A good PMO surfaces decisions, not data. We design the cadence around the choices leadership actually needs to make, and ruthlessly cut the rest.

ii.

Single source of truth

One plan, one risk register, one financial view — owned by the PMO, trusted by the workstreams, and accessible to the executive sponsor. No parallel realities.

iii.

Embedded, not parallel

We work as part of the client team, not alongside it. Our consultants chair the meetings, own the deliverables, and earn the relationships that make the function stick after we leave.
