Practice areas  /  Post-Merger Integration & Carve-out

Where deal value is made — or lost.

Most transactions deliver less value than the deal model promised. The reason is rarely the thesis; it is the execution. PAISS partners with acquirers, sellers, and management teams to make integrations and separations move with the pace and discipline that protects deal value.

The integration moment

The period between signing and Day 365 is the most demanding in a corporate's lifecycle. Two operating models meet. Customers, employees, regulators, and counterparties all want certainty. Leadership attention is finite, and the cost of every delayed decision compounds.

A well-run integration is built around three forces: a clear target operating model that everyone is moving towards, a decision cadence that matches the pace of the business, and a relentless link between every workstream and the value it is supposed to deliver. Carve-outs are the mirror image — the same disciplines, applied in reverse.

PAISS engages at every phase: from pre-close planning under clean-team conditions, through day-one readiness, to the full integration or separation programme and the synergy capture that follows.

How we engage

Across the deal lifecycle — from pre-signing readiness to post-close value capture — we provide the senior integration leadership, programme infrastructure, and cross-functional rigour that complex transactions require.

i.
Pre-close planning
Integration or separation blueprinting between signing and close. Target operating model design, day-one scoping, value baseline, clean-team support, and the cadence and governance that the post-close programme will run on. The work that determines whether Day 1 lands.
ii.
Day-one readiness
The detailed, function-by-function plan for the first 24 to 72 hours: legal entity, customer continuity, employee communications, IT cutover, finance close, vendor notifications, and the contingency book for the things that will not go to plan. Tested, not assumed.
iii.
Integration or Separation Management Office
The IMO or SMO as the operating heart of the programme — workstream architecture, cross-functional dependency management, executive cadence, value tracking, and risk visibility. Run by us, with the client, for the duration of the integration or separation.
iv.
Synergy capture & value tracking
A direct line from the deal model to weekly delivery: synergy initiatives sized, owned, sequenced, baselined, and tracked through to bookable run-rate impact. The discipline that turns announced synergies into reported ones.
v.
Carve-out & TSA management
Separation planning, stand-alone operating model design, and the negotiation and active management of Transition Services Agreements — including the often-underestimated work of exiting them on time, on cost, and without disruption.

Our approach

Three principles that distinguish PAISS integration work from generic programme management.

i.

Decision velocity

Deals fail when decisions stall. We design the integration cadence and governance to surface the right decisions to the right people fast — and to take the routine ones off leadership's desk entirely.

ii.

Value-linked workplan

Every workstream traces to a number in the deal model. If an initiative does not link to value, customer impact, or risk, it does not belong on the critical path. This single test reshapes how integrations are run.

iii.

Cross-border fluency

Our team works across Israeli, Nordic, and broader European contexts in English, Hebrew, and Swedish. The cultural and regulatory nuance of cross-border deals is part of the engagement, not an afterthought.
