Practice areas  /  CIO Office

Letting the CIO lead — not firefight.

The modern CIO sits at the intersection of business strategy, technology debt, vendor sprawl, and cyber risk. PAISS partners with technology leaders to give the office of the CIO the strategic clarity, operating discipline, and senior bandwidth it needs to make that intersection work.

The CIO's challenge

The role has changed. A CIO is now expected to be a business leader, an architect, a regulator-liaison, a vendor negotiator, a cyber custodian, a talent magnet, and an integration partner to every major business initiative. Few have the bench depth to do all of it well at once.

The office of the CIO is what makes the role survivable. It is the function — sometimes a team, sometimes a virtual one — that owns IT strategy, governance, performance management, financial discipline, and the relationship between technology and the rest of the business.

PAISS supports CIOs at the moments where that office most needs reinforcement: setting strategy in a new role, redesigning the IT operating model, rationalising the vendor portfolio, or absorbing the technology shock of a major transaction.

How we engage

From strategy through operating model, governance, and execution support — we engage where the CIO needs senior partnership and where business outcomes depend on technology getting it right.

i.
IT strategy & roadmap
A clear, business-anchored IT strategy with a sequenced, fundable roadmap. Built around the outcomes the business needs, not around technology categories. Suitable as a new-CIO 100-day output, a board-ready document, or an annual reset.
ii.
IT operating model & organisation design
Designing or redesigning the structure of the IT function — capability map, sourcing strategy, role definitions, governance forums, and decision rights. We design for the business the organisation is becoming, not the one it has outgrown.
iii.
Technology governance
Frameworks, forums, and rituals that make architecture decisions, investment decisions, and cyber risk decisions traceable, defensible, and timely. Light enough to use; rigorous enough to stand up to audit and to the board.
iv.
Vendor & portfolio rationalisation
A structured assessment of the application and supplier portfolio — consolidation opportunities, renegotiation leverage, end-of-life sequencing, and a defensible business case for change. Often the fastest path to releasing cost and complexity.
v.
CIO advisory partnership
An ongoing senior partner to the CIO — a sounding board for strategic decisions, a second pair of hands on board papers, and a discreet challenge function on the issues that are difficult to raise inside the organisation.

Our approach

Three principles that shape how we work alongside CIOs and their teams.

i.

Business outcomes first

Every recommendation we make is anchored in a business outcome — revenue, cost, risk, speed, or experience. Technology is the lever, not the headline.

ii.

Technically fluent

Our consultants come from technology backgrounds and can engage credibly with architects, security leads, and engineering managers. We do not need to be translated.

iii.

Pragmatic, not theoretical

We design for the organisation in front of us — its talent, its legacy, its culture. The best operating model is the one the organisation can actually run.
